Recurrence Quantification Analysis (RQA)

RQA

Proposed in 1987 by Eckmann, Kamphorst & Ruelle; further developed in 1992 by Zbilut & Weber

Measures the recurrence of a system under a chosen threshold parameter $\varepsilon$

We generate a binary matrix $R$, which can be represented visually (recurrence plot)

  • Eckmann, Kamphorst, Ruelle, "Recurrence Plots of Dynamical Systems", Europhysics Letters 4(9), 1987
  • Zbilut, Weber, "Embeddings and delays as derived from quantification of recurrence plots", Physics Letters A, 171(3–4), 1992

RQA

We generate a binary matrix $R$, which can be represented visually (recurrence plot)

For a norm in the phase space $\lVert \cdot \rVert$ (z.B. eukl., $\mathbb{R}^n$), def.

\[R_{ij}\coloneqq \Theta (\varepsilon - \lVert x_i - x_j \rVert) = \begin{cases} 1, &\text{wenn } \lVert x_i-x_j\rVert \leq \varepsilon \\ 0, &\text{wenn } \lVert x_i-x_j \rVert > \varepsilon \end{cases}\]
Where $\Theta$ is the Heaviside Function

    Cons:
  • Full matrix requires $\Rightarrow \mathcal{O}(n^2)$ computation time
  • Doesn't work well for all data types
  • Schultz, Spiegel, Marwan, Albayrak, "Approximation of diagonal line based measures in recurrence quantification analysis"

Quantification(s) for RQA

Various measures, e.g.:

  • Recurrence rate $RR = \frac{1}{N^2}\sum_{i,j=1}^N R_{i,j}$
  • Determinism $DET = \frac{\sum_{l=l_{\min}}^N l P(l)}{\sum_{l=1}^N l P(l)}$
  • Laminarity $LAM = \frac{\sum_{v=v_{\min}}^N v P(v)}{\sum_{v=1}^N v P(v)}$
  • Entropy $ENTR = -\sum_{l=l_{\min}}^N p(l) \ln p(l)$
  • Marwan, "Quantification of Recurrence Plots"recurrence-plot.tk
  • N. Marwan, M. C. Romano, M. Thiel, J. Kurths: Recurrence Plots for the Analysis of Complex Systems, Physics Reports, 438(5-6), 237-329, 2007.

Microstates

For an $m < n$ (i.d.R. $m \ll n$, z.B. 2, 3) choose $m \times m$ section of $R$ and consider distribution thereof in $R$

  • Statistical measure, quantifies system state
  • Sparse sampling reduces computational time strongly

Event-Serien

Event-Serien

Für Ereignisse mit Binärzuständen (Klimakatastrophen, Aktionspotenziale, Herzrhythmusstörungen), d.h. Daten von Form $(t_i,x_i), x_i\in\{0,1\}$, bzw. $(t_i)$

$\Rightarrow$ Direkte RQA nicht geeignet; trotzdem wollen wir Zeitabschnitte vergleichen

Für regelmäßig gesamplete Daten, passt RQA gut, aber dies ist selten der Fall; Zeiteinbettung geht auch gut, aber fehlende Daten sind sehr störend
